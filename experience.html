My Working Experiences

I finished my SPM in 2016 and I remember the joy of sitting at home thinking about never having homework again!
While waiting for my SPM results to release, I tried to be productive
by looking for a part-time job to earn some extra cash for the time being.
I got hired at a bakery near my house area and with no experience I learned quickly
that it would be hard. Working in a bakery might be joyful but it was also tough.
The bakery opens well before the morning rush and the employees start baking even before that.
At 17, I was working six days a week, tired and unimpressed with my career.



Every morning, I would pack orders for the day, put fresh bread out
and make sure the display cases were all stocked and clean.
Like any other job, it has its ups and downs. I was working 8 hours days
even during weekends. I barely had enough time to hang out with friends, or even watch TV.
Everytime after work, my back and knees hurts so bad so I will immediately washed up and go
to sleep. It was tiring, but despite it all, it was satisfying. I also learned on how to multitask
by remembering the recipes. With that skills, I used it to bake cake for my family sometimes.
When I first received my first official pay-check and although it was not that much,
I also learned on how to be professional working with a team of people, some of whom I consider good friends to this day.


After 5 months working at the bakery, I decided to resigned and looked out for other part-time job.
I got hired at a Cotton On, Aeon Mall Bukit Mertajam as a Sales Assistant.
My daily job is to greet customers who enter the shop, always responsible for ensuring that customers
have an enjoyable shopping experience and spends lots of cash so the business can keep going.
Also, in total charge of unpacking clothes, hanging them and then security tagging them.
Honestly, I can say that I loved working here compare to the bakery because it is less tiring
and I got a day off per week. As a part-timer, I can say that it is a great job for me to earn
some extra money and also I was able to get a fun team although we have to handle some difficult customers.
However, we have good communication, cooperation and teamwork to increase the store's sale.
Besides, I also learned a lot of skills on how to manage the store and customers.
I'll always remember my bakery time as tough, hard work, but it's also a full, lived experience for me.


My Volunteering Experience

In 2018, for my community engagement project I decided to joined my father to do the volunteering work at
Pattani, Thailand. I didn't know much about them or what I did when I first joined them. When I was volunteering,
part of what I did was get the donations from people and sort them into the proper places.
It was important for me to see and feel how people in other cultures lives. I always
find it amazing how friendly and helpful most poor people are. While doing this project,
I learned a lot about the Thai's community. First of all, even though I knew that Pattani was an
area that had an issue with poverty, I didn't realize how bad it was in places.
This charity project also showed me that when a community works together they can have great results.
After that, I decided to continue doing a lot of community service. I have done some
charity project during my Diploma year with my friends to help the strays cats and
also donating some food supplies and clothes to the orphans. This community project made
me leave my comfort zone a bit. I had a lot of fun volunteering with young children and animals.
Overall, it was an amazing experiences for me and I feel like my time was well spent. I also feel that
without these experiences, I may missed out on something huge.
